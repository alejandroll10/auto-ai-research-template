## Domain: Finance Theory

### Asset pricing
- No-arbitrage / SDF framework as benchmark
- Risk premia must have an economic explanation
- Connection to observable objects (factors, portfolios, returns)
- Key question: what risk is being priced, and why?

### Corporate finance
- Modigliani-Miller as benchmark (what friction breaks it?)
- Standard frictions: agency, asymmetric info, incomplete contracts
- One friction at a time for clarity
- Key question: what friction matters, and what does it imply?

### Information economics and market microstructure
- Rational expectations equilibrium (Grossman-Stiglitz) as benchmark
- Information aggregation: when do prices reveal private information?
- Market microstructure: order flow, bid-ask spreads, price discovery
- Prediction markets: aggregation, manipulation, liquidity, market design
- Key question: how does information get into prices, and what distorts it?

### Market design and mechanism design
- Auction theory (Myerson, Milgrom-Weber) as benchmark
- Optimal market structure: continuous vs. batch, lit vs. dark
- Information disclosure: what should be public vs. private?
- Platform design: matching, pricing, fee structure
- Key question: what institution or mechanism achieves the desired outcome?

### Financial intermediation
- Diamond-Dybvig, Holmström-Tirole as benchmarks
- Banks, shadow banks, funds — who intermediates and why?
- Moral hazard in delegation: risk-shifting, leverage, skin in the game
- Systemic risk: interconnection, contagion, too-big-to-fail
- Key question: why do intermediaries exist, and what are the consequences?

### Behavioral and belief-based finance
- Rational benchmark: what does the standard model predict?
- Heterogeneous beliefs, overconfidence, extrapolation, attention
- Belief-driven trade volume, volatility, bubbles
- Key question: what happens to equilibrium when beliefs deviate from rational?
